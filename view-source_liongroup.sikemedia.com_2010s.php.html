<h4>Instagram</h4>
<p>In 2010, Kevin Systrom and Mike Krieger launched an application for the iPhone for photo and video sharing, called Instagram. The app allows users to upload photos and videos, which can be edited using filters. Within two months of its launch, Instagram gained over one million users. In April 2012, Instagram released a version for Android devices, followed by a feature-limited web interface releasing in November. Instagram has blown up in popularity over the years with it reaching over 800 million users as of September 2017.</p>
<img src="images/instagram.png" class="img-fluid rounded mx-auto d-block" />
<br />
<h4>Snapchat</h4>
<p>Snapchat is a multimedia messaging app that allows users to send photos and videos that are only available for a short amount of time before they become inaccessible. Initially, the app launched in July 2011 as "Picaboo" for iOS devices. The app was relaunched in September 2011 for iOS as Snapchat. In May 2012, there were approximately 25 images being sent per second, and by November, over one billion photos had been shared on the iOS app. In October 2012, Snapchat launched for Android devices. Snapchat reached 160 million daily active users in February 2017, growing to 166 million in May.</p>
