<div class="row">
<div class="col-lg-8 mx-auto">
<p>In the 1990s, a technological boom started to happen which lead us into the modern times. The first modems began to hit our personal pcs which led to an online revolution.</p>
<img src="images/pcimodems.png" class="img-fluid rounded mx-auto d-block" />
<br />
<h4>PCI Modems</h4>
<p>Modems at speeds of 28.8 kbps or 33.6 kbps started to become so integral that they were being shipped within the pcs of the 90s. This led to the rush of email, electronic BBS, and AOL. I believe this is the official start of the online age. These modems required a phone line and allowed users to send faxes from their own PCs. Modems that would allow voice calls to be made electronically also first appeared in the mid-1990s.</p>
<img src="images/bbs.png" class="img-fluid rounded mx-auto d-block" />
<br />
<h4>Electronic BBS (Bulletin Board System)</h4>
<p>Along with the rise in speed and increase of personal use modems came the Electronic BBS. The modem would dial in to a server that would allow users to chat, as well as provide online gaming and file leeching capabilities. They used text graphics called ANSII which provided a robust GUI for the user to navigate by keyboard.</p>
<img src="images/aol.png" class="img-fluid rounded mx-auto d-block" />
<br />
<h4>America Online</h4>
<p>Even though they began to flourish after CompuServe and Prodigy existed, America Online would perhaps be remembered as the first major paid online services. America Online, as we know it, began in 1991 and started mailing CD-ROMs with free trials for every household. This led to a membership lasting about 2 years and costing about 350 dollars for the average user. They charged an hourly fee until around 1996 when they changed to a flat monthly rate of $19.95. America Online can be remembered for giving us the first mainstream email service of the "You've got Mail" age, the first online homework help service, news service, and perhaps the most popular innovation of Instant Messaging. Instant messaging has outlived AOL as a major internet provider and is used in almost every social network today.</p>
<img src="images/2waypagers.png" class="img-fluid rounded mx-auto d-block" />
<br />
<h4>2-Way Pagers</h4>
<p>2-Way pagers gained much popularity in the 90s. It took the pager game up a step by allowing a message to be sent and answered without the use of a telephone. These were no longer just for the professional and were especially popular with those in their teens and early 20s.</p>
<img src="images/cellphones.png" class="img-fluid rounded mx-auto d-block" />
<br />
<h4>Cell Phones</h4>
<p>Cell phones decreased to pocket size and were no longer just for the profession on the go. The shift in advertising began to market cell phones to everyone and would cost an average of $200 including airtime. These early cellphones didn't have text or internet, but were still a huge step in what cell phones would eventually become.</p>
