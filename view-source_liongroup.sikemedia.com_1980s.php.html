<h4></h4>
<p>The 80's was a decade of technological breakthroughs. While the first PCs were entering homes, the internet and emails were not common place except by the military and government. However, many communication devices were available to many personal users for the first time.</p>
<img src="images/faxmachine.png" class="img-fluid rounded mx-auto d-block" />
<br />
<h4>The Desktop Fax Machine</h4>
<p>Since e-mails and internet were not available, the desktop fax machine made it possible for users to send documents to each other using a standard dedicated telephone line. This marvel made office life in the 80s much simpler to share photos, documents, and business secrets from anywhere in the world if they had telephone service.</p>
<img src="images/cellphone.png" class="img-fluid rounded mx-auto d-block" />
<br />
<h4>Cellular Phones</h4>
<p>The first cell phones came out in the mid-80's. The devices were roughly the size of a brick and cost upwards of $4000. Also, call cost was more than a dollar a minute. These were mainly used by stockbrokers or business men who lost money by not being available at any given minute.</p>
<img src="images/cordlessphones.png" class="img-fluid rounded mx-auto d-block" />
<br />
<h4>Cordless Telephones</h4>
<p>Cordless telephone began a rise of popularity in the 80s. For the first time, someone could walk around there home freely while talking on the telephone instead of being at the mercy of a wire. These first coreless phones had rather awful reception, battery life and cost in the range of $250-$400, but the freedom of mobility these offered were worth it for most.</p>
<img src="images/pagers.png" class="img-fluid rounded mx-auto d-block" />
<br />
<h4>One-Way Pagers</h4>
<p>These devices could be seen on doctors, repairmen, and professionals everywhere in the 80s. A number would come through and then the party paged would have to call the number sent to find out more about the situation. </p>
